# Modeling-Friendship-Formation-in-Highschool
The project uses Exponential Random Graph Models (ERGMs) to calculate the probability of friendship between two students given a certain attribute.  

High school societies are networks where the complex nature of human societies and relationships are clearly represented. The most fundamental building block of these networks is friendships, connections that ultimately lead to the networks' overall social organization. Nonetheless, few studies have analyzed friendship formation patterns in schools outside the US, and most studies use a primarily local demographic. This study answers this gap by conducting a social network analysis (SNA) in an international high school in Mexico, where a network of 140 students was analyzed. Using Exponential Random Graph Models (ERGMs), the study was able to calculate the probability of friendship formation given demographic attributes and extracurricular participation. Results found that having shared friends (transitivity), race, and gender increased the likelihood of friendship formation. On the other hand, extracurricular co-participation was not found as a strong predictor of friendships. The conclusions of this study provide a new understanding of how friendships form in international high schools outside the US.
